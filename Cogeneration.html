What is cogeneration?

Cogeneration is a promising green technology that makes use of "waste" heat in two ways. A cogeneration plant uses waste heat to produce electricity onsite and also to heat or cool the building.

How does cogeneration compare to typical heat engines?

Typical heat engines can produce electricity but simply reject waste heat to the atmosphere. By using cogeneration as opposed to typical heat engines, efficiency is increased from 40% to 80%.

How does cogeneration work?

The cogeneration plant runs on air and natural gas to produce electricity as well as "waste" heat. In winter, this waste heat from the cogeneration plant is passed through a heat exchanger for heating. During the summer, this heat is used to drive an absorption chiller. The heat exchanger and chiller then help to regulate the temperature of the building through the radiant panels.
